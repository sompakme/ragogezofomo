Missy Gold biography, boyfriend or husband and family

In the Hollywood industry, we have seen many child stars who have become celebrities while others fade into obscurity. Those in the latter category end up pursuing a career in a different field and Missy Gold happens to be one of them. The American actress rose to fame as a child star and is best known for her iconic roles in projects such as Benson, Fantasy Island, and Nancy Drew Mysteries, among others.

In her heyday, Missy enjoyed a burgeoning acting career. After making her acting debut in 1976, the actress was active in the industry for just over a decade and her final role was on the long-running series, Benson. I guess you are curious to know more about this thespian? Check out more interesting facts about Missy below, including her biography and family life.

Missy Gold – Biography

The blonde actress was born on July 14, 1970. Although her birth name was Melissa Fisher, she is known as Missy Gold. Gold was born to Bonnie and Joe Fisher, originally from Great Falls, Montana.

Missy’s fondness for the performing arts as a child led her to pursue a career in acting. She made her acting debut in 1976, at the young age of six. Her first screen role was in a miniseries Captains and Kings where she played the role of Mary Armagh. The following year she starred as Judy Cardler in Rafferty and as Grace in The Hardy Boys/Nancy Drew Mysteries.

In 1978, she appeared in four television shows including Little Mo, Sword of Justice, The Kid from Not-So-Big, and Ishi: The Last of His Tribe. The previous year, she was seen in Eight is Enough, Project UFO, and How the West Was Won. In the early 80s, Missy Gold appeared in Fantasy Island as Stephanie Hendricks; in Trapper John, MD as Rachel McCall, and she voiced Blink in The Blinkins: The Bear and the Blizzard

She had her breakthrough in 1979 when she joined the cast of the American sitcom, Benson. In the series, she portrayed the role of Katie Gatling, the Governor’s daughter alongside James Noble and Robert Guillaume. For her wonderful performance in the series, Missy was awarded with an award for Best Young Actress in a Comedy Series in 1984. Shortly after the show ended in 1986, Missy decided to quit acting in order to pursue her studies. .

Acting career

After retiring, the actress proceeded to Georgetown University, where she was pre-med. Shortly after graduation, she went on to acquire her doctorate. from the California School of Professional Psychology.

The actress-turned-psychologist is currently stealing her trade in St. Helena, California and is now known as Dr. Melissa Gold Weidmann. She has also received other training such as pre-doctoral training at the Los Angeles Police Department as well as Behavioral Science Service and post-doctoral training at the Rape Treatment Center at the University of California.

Family

Missy Gold came from a middle-class American family. She is the daughter of advertising executive Bonnie Fisher and her husband, Joe Fisher. The actress was raised with her sister, Tracey Gold, who is also an actress. Tracey is best remembered for her role as Carol Seaver on the 80s sitcom, Growing Pains

When they were very young, Missy’s parents divorced. Following this, their mom, Bonnie, later married a man named Harry Goldstein who would later adopt Missy and Tracey. Although they changed their surname from Fisher to Goldstein, Missy and her siblings later shortened it to Gold for work purposes.

Thanks to their mother’s second marriage to Harry, Missy also has three other younger half-siblings. They include Cassandra, Brandy and Jessica Gold. Two of her three half-siblings – Brandy and Jessie have established themselves as competent actresses in Hollywood.

Brandy is famous for her role in the NBC medical drama series St. Elsewhere while Jessie is known for appearing in films such as The Longest Week and Eating Out series, among others. Cassandra is the only member of their family to follow a different career path. However, there aren’t many details regarding Cassie and what she does for a living.
