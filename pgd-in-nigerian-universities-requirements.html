PGD in Nigerian Universities: requirements

If you would like to further your studies, then you should know the requirements for PGD in Nigerian Universities. Let's check the general requirements for the programmes!

PGD Degree

This kind of degree is for students who have already concluded their Bachelor Degree programmes. They can desire to continue their education and become better specialists in their professions. They may want to go into the field of science or become lecturers in universities.

Nigeria can offer various choices for students to get their PGD diploma. However, to obtain this diploma, students must follow certain requirements. Keep in mind that a person can continue his or her postgraduate study only in the area where he/she holds the Bachelor degree (or its equivalent).

Postgraduate Diploma requirements

  • The graduates from the accredited universities in Nigeria can apply for any PGD form in a Nigerian University.
  • Holders of other alternatives to the Bachelor Degree qualifications, like C.P.A, BEEC., ICAN, ACMA, ACCA, A.I.B. can also apply and be considered for the PGD admission.
  • All candidates with professional qualifications (HND) must obtain five credits in GCE or WASC, including English Language.
  • Do not forget that you can only apply for areas of study that are relevant to your degree.
  • Every faculty also has the right to conduct examinations and receive students according to the results of these examinations. Therefore, be prepared.

Master`s Degree

Master Degree programme is another set of courses provided at the postgraduate degree level in Nigeria. Graduates from recognized universities in the country are more than welcome to apply for a Masters Degree. Who can join this programme?

  • Graduates who hold at least a Bachelor Degree or relevant postgraduate diploma;
  • To apply for the master degree, you must have at least a second class upper degree in your Postgraduate Diploma Education. You should have at least four years of the studentship experience;
  • Graduates who hold professional Fellowship qualifications, like NIRP, FNILT or FIMLT can also apply for the Masters degree programme.

Doctorate Degree

This is another step in the PGD degree in Nigeria. It's higher than the Masters Degree so it requires a lot more effort. Candidates with Masters Degree from recognized universities can apply for the doctorate or Ph.D. Programmes.

Every university in Nigeria has its own sets of rules to become doctors. The general requirement to be applicable for the examination is to obtain a grade point average of at least 3.5 out of 5.

Ph.D. is the highest degree awarded in the most of countries. It will give you the opportunity to work as university professors, scientists or researchers.

The General Rules for all PGD Degree

Don't forget that you may need to go through a test or examination process when you apply to any of these programmes. You should also provide your Diplomas from previous universities. Nigerian Universities can accept Diplomas from the universities of other countries. However, you can contact the special commissions responsible for new applicants. They will give you the full list of documents needed to apply for the University.
