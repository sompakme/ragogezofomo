Known professionally as Jimmy Buffett, James William Buffett is a well-known American musician, film producer, performer, songwriter, and author who comes from the state of Florida. Jimmy Buffett is best known to the general public as a member of “The Coral Reefer Band,” which serves as Buffett’s travelling and recording band. Paul McCartney, Steve Goodman, Rick Flair, Harrison Ford, and Clint Black are just a few of the honorary special guests that have performed on stage with “The Coral Reefer Band” throughout the course of the band’s history. His first studio album, “Down to Earth,” was released in 1970, and it was the inspiration for such hit songs as “The Christian?” and “Ellis Dee,” as well as the film “Captain America.” Jimmy Buffett’s career as a musician began in the 1960s. 

The songs “Come Monday” and “Margaritaville” are among the most well-known that Buffett has released throughout the course of his long career. The song “Come Monday,” which was published in 1974, was Buffett’s first to chart in the Top 40 of the Billboard Hot 100, peaking at #30 on the list. In 1977, Buffett released a song called “Margaritaville,” which became instantly famous and quickly became one of the most popular songs in the world. In addition to being endorsed by celebrities such as The Rock and Alan Jackson, the song has become a popular brand name for a variety of products, including “Margaritaville Tequila,” “Margaritaville Men’s and Women’s Apparel,” “Margaritaville margarita mix,” and “Margaritaville Outdoor & Beach Furniture,” to name a few.
