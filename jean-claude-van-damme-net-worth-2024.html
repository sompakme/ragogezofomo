Early Life and Career Beginnings

Jean-Claude Van Damme was born in Berchem-Sainte-Agathe, Brussels, Belgium. He began martial arts training at the age of 10, focusing on Shotokan karate and kickboxing. His early career was marked by success in martial arts competitions, which eventually led to his breakthrough in Hollywood.

Hollywood Success

Van Damme’s Hollywood career began in the 1980s with minor roles, but his breakthrough came with the film “Bloodsport” in 1988. His unique blend of martial arts and acting skills quickly made him a sought-after action star. His subsequent films, including “Kickboxer,” “Universal Soldier,” and “Timecop,” further cemented his status in Hollywood.
