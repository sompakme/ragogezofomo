Why does my LG Dryer says check filter?

Answer

In the case of an LG dryer, the check filter light does not illuminate when the filter is clogged or overloaded. The light illuminates every time the dryer is turned on, serving as a visual reminder to empty the lint filter. By pressing the “Start/Pause” button on the control panel, the check filter light can be turned off and on again as needed.

Furthermore, what does the term “check filter” on an LG dryer mean?

The check filter indicator on the display panel was created to serve as a visual reminder to clean the lint filter before each use in order to ensure consistently excellent drying results. If there is nothing wrong with your dryer, this indicator will automatically turn off once the drying cycle has begun.

One might also wonder, how does one go about checking a dryer filter? 

Dryer filters should be tested by first cleaning them thoroughly and then pouring a small amount of water onto the filter screens. Washing the screen in warm, soapy water with a brush should resolve the problem if the water pools instead of flowing through it. Rinse, retest, and repeat the process until the water flows freely through the pipe.

Also, I’d like to know why my dryer keeps telling me to clean the filter.

Dryers with electronic controls have a “Clean Filter” reminder that appears on the screen after each cycle is completed and remains on the screen until the next load is run. Despite the fact that the lint filter has been cleaned, this is a reminder message that does not appear to be related to the sensor. It is not possible to turn it off.
