Little Big Town is a country music singing group from the United States. Since its formation in 1998, the band has consisted of the same four members: Karen Fairchild, Kimberly Schlapman, Jimi Westbrook, and Phillip Sweet. The Mamas and the Papas’ and Peter, Paul and Mary’s musical styles both rely extensively on four-part vocal harmonies, with all four members rotating as lead vocalists. Sweet and Westbrook both play rhythm guitar. Little Big Town launched their self-titled debut on Monument Records in 2002, after a recording contract with Mercury Nashville Records that yielded no singles or albums. Before leaving the label, the group had two modest country chart songs. By 2005, the band had signed with Equity Music Group, Clint Black’s independent record company.  That same year, their second album, The Road to Here, was released. It was certified platinum in the United States and spawned two Top Ten country songs in “Boondocks” and “Bring It On Home.” Their third studio album, A Place to Land, was released in 200″I’m with the Band,” the album’s first song, was a Top 40 success on the country charts. The group was signed to Capitol Records Nashville shortly after its release, which purchased the rights to A Place to Land and released “Fine Line” and “Good Lord Willing” as the album’s second and third singles, respectively. The latter song is included in the re-release for the first time. The Reason Why, their first Capitol CD, was released in 2010.
