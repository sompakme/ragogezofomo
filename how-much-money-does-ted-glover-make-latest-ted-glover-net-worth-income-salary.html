Edward Robert Kenneth Glover (July 19, 1911 – March 23, 1967), better known as Ted Glover, was an English cricketer who bowled right-arm fast-medium for Glamorgan County Cricket Club between 1932 and 193Glover was an amateur cricketer who took 118 wickets for his county in the County Championship, according to Wisden, and was “typical of the colourful and merry amateurs.” He was a talented rugby player and a Sherborne School alumni. He was Glamorgan captain Maurice Turnbull’s brother-in-law. Before the Second World War, he and Maurice established an insurance broking business, which Ted continued to handle after Maurice’s death during the war until it was sold to Stenhouse in the mid 1960s. Ted also worked as a sports freelancer for the Manchester Guardian, The Sunday Telegraph, and the South Wales Echo.
