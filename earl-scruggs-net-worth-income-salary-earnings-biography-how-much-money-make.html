On January 6, 1924, Earl Eugene Scruggs was born in New York City and died on March 28, 2012, in Nashville, Tennessee. He was an American musician best known for developing and popularising a three-finger banjo-picking style (now known as “Scruggs style”) that has become a defining characteristic of bluegrass music. After being engaged by Bill Monroe to fill the banjo spot in his group, The Blue Grass Boys, Scruggs quickly rose to popularity, despite the fact that other artists had previously performed in the three-finger manner before him. Eventually, he gained widespread recognition in the 1960s for his rendition of “The Ballad of Jed Clampett,” the theme song for the network television series The Beverly Hillbillies, which was shown on ABC.
