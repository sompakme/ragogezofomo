Jaylon Smith Profile, Brother, Girlfriend, Height, Weight, Body Stats

Jaylon Smith is an American linebacker for the Dallas Cowboys of the NFL. He started his professional career in 2016 after a successful college career at the University of Notre Dame. Jaylon started playing football in high school, where he was double-and-a-half as a second, leading Rivals.com and Scout.com to rank him the best outside linebacker in his class.

The famous athlete was drafted into the National Football League in the 2nd round of the 2016 NFL Draft by the Dallas Cowboys. Since then, the talented footballer has been playing for the Dallas Cowboys alongside his older brother, Rod, who is a running back for the team.

Profile and biography of Jaylon Smith

The famous footballer was born in Fort Wayne, Indiana on June 14, 1995. He is the second son of Roger Smith and Sophia Woodson, who have two other children, Saniya Smith and Rod Smith.

The American athlete attended Bishop Luers High School, Fort Wayne where he competed in basketball, football and track and field. Jaylon won several honors and accolades in all three matches but he was more prominent in football where he recorded astonishing career stats. This led him to play football at the college level and was eventually selected by the NFL in the second round of the 2016 draft. He signed a 4-year rookie contract worth $6.49 million dollars with a signing bonus of $2.9 million.

Jaylon Smith’s Brother: Rod Smith

Dallas Cowboys running back Rod Smith is Jaylon Smith’s older brother. The two brothers attended different high schools and once faced each other. The match which was won by Jaylon’s team with a score of 14-8 gave him the eternal bragging rights of his big brother.

Jaylon said watching his older brother achieve fame and success made him want to succeed as well. Like all younger brothers, he always strived to be better than his big brother and step out of his shadow.

Their father, Roger Smith, describes his two sons as part of the same team, a source of pride for his family. According to Roger, it’s a bit surreal, especially since the Dallas Cowboys are his favorite NFL team. What more could a father ask for than his boys to have the opportunity to achieve their dreams and aspirations?
