Jordy Nelson: biography, family, education

The athlete was born on May 31, 1985 in Manhattan, Kansas, United States as Jordy Ray Nelson. He was born the son of parents Alan and Kim Nelson, the farmers lived in Riley. His great-great-grandparents have immigrated from Sweden. He has two brothers: Kelsey and Mike Nelson.

For his education, he completed his elementary school at Riley County High School in Riley where he started playing football. He had played quarterback during his senior year. He completed 62 percent of his passes for 1,029 yards, 8 touchdowns and rushed for 1,572 yards. In that period, he also performed in track and field where he won an AAU National Championship in the 400 meters. He then went on to join Kansas State University and played with coach Bill Synder’s Wildcats from 2003 to 2005. And he played his bye college seasons with Kansas State (2006 and 2007).

Jordy Nelson: Career and achievements in life

In the 2008 NFL Draft, he was selected in the second round by the Green Bay Packers with the 36th pick. He was one of two Kansas State Wildcats selected that year. He then signed his rookie contract with the team on July 27, 2008. He debuted his NFL game of the season against the Minnesota Vikings. In 2010, Nelson caught a 29-yard touchdown pass on third-and-1 to Steeler William Gay covering the first touchdown of Super Bowl XLV.

Additionally, he finished his 2011 regular season with a career high of 15 touchdowns, 68 receptions, and 1,263 receiving yards. In 2012, he missed all four of his games due to a hamstring injury. Later, he along with his team finished with a record of 11-5 and won the NFC North. He was then named the Pro-Bowl alternate in the 2013 season game, where he started all 16 games for the first time in his career. In the same year, he was ranked 83rd by his teammates on the Top 100 NFL Players of 2014.

In 2018, he signed a two-year, $15 million contract with the Oakland Raiders in March. He finished his game of the season second on the team in receiving yards and touchdowns. On March 14, 2019, the Raiders released him after one season. However, he announced his retirement from the NFL after playing 11 seasons in the same month. He then signed a one-day deal to retire as a Green Bay Packer player in August 2019.
